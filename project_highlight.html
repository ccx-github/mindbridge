Brain-computer interface--Steady state visual evoked potential

A steady-state visual evoked potential is a type of visual evoked potential that occurs when the human eye is staring at a constant frequency flashing visual stimulus When the source (such as a 10Hz light flicker), the brain corresponds to it will produce at the same frequency or its harmonics (e.g 10Hz, 20Hz, etc.) at the energy prominent brain electrical response. Because it is located in the occipital lobe of the brain (visual processing), it is convenient for non-invasive EEG sensors to collect EEG electrical signals. And it has high stability and recognition, because it is the only choice for EEG recognition. In this project, we use it to identify which key the user focuses on, which is the core technology to realize the input.

Brain-computer interface--ICA (Independent Component Analysis)

Independent Component Analysis (ICA) is a blind source separation algorithm that is used when multiple channels of EEG signals are mixed simultaneously When it comes to sources (such as eye movements, muscle activity, signals from specific areas of the brain), it can use mathematical decomposition to split these mixed components into statistically independent source signals. Because it can significantly weaken or remove blinking artifacts and myoelectric interference, it is convenient for subsequent algorithms to extract more pure EEG signals closely related to target tasks (such as SSVEP, motor imagination, etc.). It can improve signal-to-noise ratio and stability, so it is an important method for EEG artifact removal and feature extraction. In this project, we use it to identify and separate the noise signal from the brain electrical activity of real interest, which is a key means to ensure the quality of the core signal.

Brain-computer interface--IIR (Infinite Impulse Response) filter

Infinite length impulse response filter (IIR) is a kind of digital filter. When we need to perform fast bandpass or notch filtering for real-time EEG signal, the output feedback structure is introduced in IIR, which can realize steep frequency selection characteristics at relatively low order. Because of its high computational efficiency and the ability to effectively reduce the overall processing delay of the system, IIR filters have become a common choice for many online BCI systems, especially for suppressing power frequency interference or other unwanted frequency band energy in real-time scenarios. In this project, we use it to filter multi-channel EEG signals in real time to filter out common noise and Preserve the characteristics of the target band to further improve recognition accuracy and stability.
